🌱 Sorghum - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Sorghum thrives in black and red soil with good drainage. It can also grow in slightly acidic soils.

☀️ Climate: Sorghum is a drought-resistant crop and grows well in hot climates with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 35°C.

💧 Water Requirements: Sorghum is drought-tolerant but requires irrigation in dry periods. Over-watering should be avoided.

📅 Growing Season: Grown in the Kharif season, sowing takes place in June and July. Harvesting is done in October–November, typically after 4–5 months.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Sorghum matures in about 120–150 days.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Sorghum is susceptible to aphids, stem borer, and fungal diseases like rust and smut.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Apply a balanced NPK fertilizer in the ratio of 40:20:20. Use organic manure to improve soil quality.

🌾 Average Yield: Average yield is around 10–12 quintals per hectare under good management practices.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Use certified seeds for high germination rates.
  • Control weeds and pests early to prevent damage.
  • Provide proper irrigation during dry spells.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to reduce competition.
