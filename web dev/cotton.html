🌱 Cotton - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Cotton grows best in deep black soil with good drainage. It also does well in loamy soil that is rich in organic matter.

☀️ Climate: Cotton needs a warm climate. The ideal temperature is between 21°C to 30°C. It requires plenty of sunshine during its growing period.

💧 Water Requirements: Cotton needs moderate water. It can tolerate dry conditions but cannot survive waterlogging. Irrigation is necessary in areas with less rainfall.

📅 Growing Season: Cotton is usually grown in the Kharif season. Sowing is done between June and July, and harvesting is done after 6 to 8 months, around December to February.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Cotton takes around 160 to 180 days to mature and be ready for harvest.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Bollworms, aphids, and mealybugs are common pests. Fungal infections like wilt and leaf spots can also affect the plant. Regular monitoring and organic pesticides can help control them.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Apply nitrogen (N), phosphorus (P), and potassium (K) in balanced amounts. A general recommendation is 100:50:50 NPK per hectare. Use compost or green manure to improve soil fertility.

🌾 Average Yield: With good management, farmers can get 15–20 quintals of cotton per hectare.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Use certified and disease-resistant seeds.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants for air circulation.
  • Use drip irrigation if possible to save water.
  • Weed control during early stages improves growth.
