🌱 Cashew - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Cashew trees grow best in laterite soil with well-drained conditions. The soil should be slightly acidic and rich in organic matter for optimal growth.

☀️ Climate: Cashew requires a warm tropical climate, with temperatures ranging between 25°C to 35°C. It thrives in areas with a dry climate during the flowering period and a moderate amount of rainfall.

💧 Water Requirements: Cashew trees need moderate water. They are drought-tolerant once established, but adequate irrigation is required during the flowering and fruiting stages to ensure good yields.

📅 Growing Season: Cashew is grown primarily during the dry season, with planting in June-July and harvesting between January and March, depending on the region.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Cashew trees typically take 3–5 years to start producing nuts, with full fruiting occurring after 5–7 years of growth.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Cashew trees are prone to pests like cashew borers, root borers, and mealybugs. Fungal diseases like powdery mildew can also affect the crop.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: A balanced application of NPK fertilizers (10:10:10) is recommended. Apply organic compost to improve soil fertility and ensure healthy tree growth.

🌾 Average Yield: The yield varies based on the age of the tree, soil fertility, and climate conditions, with a mature tree yielding around 10–15 kg of cashew nuts per year.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure proper spacing between trees to allow good air circulation and reduce disease risk.
  • Regular pruning of trees helps in promoting healthy growth and better fruiting.
  • Control pests with organic methods like neem oil or other natural pesticides.
