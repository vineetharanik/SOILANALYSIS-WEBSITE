🌱 Jute - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Jute grows best in alluvial soil with high moisture retention. It prefers slightly acidic to neutral soil with good organic content and excellent drainage.

☀️ Climate: Jute requires a warm and humid climate. The ideal temperature ranges from 25°C to 35°C, with a rainfall of 1000–1500 mm annually. It thrives in areas with high humidity.

💧 Water Requirements: Jute requires abundant water, particularly during its growing period. It grows best in areas with a steady water supply, including flood-prone areas, as it needs to grow in wet conditions.

📅 Growing Season: Jute is typically grown during the Kharif season. Sowing begins in May–June, and harvesting takes place in September–October, after 4–5 months.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Jute takes around 4–5 months to mature and is ready for harvest.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Common pests include jute weevils, stem borers, and aphids. Fungal diseases like jute stem rot and bacterial leaf blight can also affect the plant.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Apply well-rotted manure or compost during land preparation. A balanced NPK fertilizer (20:10:10) can be used to support growth, with a higher dose of nitrogen for leafy growth.

🌾 Average Yield: Jute yields around 2–3 tons per hectare, depending on soil conditions, water availability, and management practices.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure the field is flooded with water during the growing season for optimum growth.
  • Plant jute in rows with adequate spacing to avoid overcrowding and ensure better sunlight exposure.
  • Regularly monitor for pests and diseases, especially during the monsoon season.
  • Use organic fertilizers and compost to enhance soil fertility and improve plant health.
