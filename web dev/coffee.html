🌱 Coffee - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Coffee plants thrive in well-drained, slightly acidic laterite soil rich in organic matter. Good drainage is crucial to prevent root rot.

☀️ Climate: Coffee grows best in tropical climates with temperatures between 18°C to 24°C. It requires a moderate amount of rainfall throughout the year with a dry period to help with flowering.

💧 Water Requirements: Coffee plants need consistent moisture, especially during the flowering and fruiting stages. However, they are susceptible to root rot if exposed to waterlogging.

📅 Growing Season: Coffee is grown year-round in regions with consistent rainfall. The flowering season typically occurs in the dry period, and the coffee cherries are harvested 7–9 months later.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Coffee trees take around 3–4 years to start bearing fruit, with peak production occurring after 6–7 years of growth.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Coffee is vulnerable to pests like coffee borer beetles and mealybugs. Fungal diseases such as coffee leaf rust are also common in humid conditions.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Regular application of organic matter and a balanced fertilizer mix (NPK 20:20:20) helps promote healthy growth and higher yields. Use slow-release fertilizers for optimal results.

🌾 Average Yield: A mature coffee tree yields about 1–5 kg of coffee cherries per year, depending on the age of the tree and environmental conditions.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Protect coffee plants from strong winds and direct sunlight by planting them under shade trees.
  • Prune coffee trees regularly to remove dead or weak branches and improve air circulation.
  • Monitor and manage pests and diseases using organic or integrated pest management techniques.
