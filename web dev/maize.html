🌽 Maize - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Maize grows best in well-drained, loamy soils with a pH range of 5.8 to 7.0. It requires rich organic content and good water-holding capacity.

☀️ Climate: Maize thrives in warm climates with temperatures ranging from 21°C to 30°C. It requires full sunlight and moderate rainfall (500–900 mm annually).

💧 Water Requirements: Maize needs consistent moisture, especially during the flowering and grain-filling stages. It is sensitive to water stress during critical stages of growth.

📅 Growing Season: Maize is typically grown during the Kharif season. Sowing begins in April–May, and harvesting takes place in September–October, after around 4–5 months.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Maize is ready for harvest after 3–4 months, depending on the variety and environmental conditions.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Pests include corn borer, armyworm, and aphids. Diseases like downy mildew and rust can also affect the crop.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Use a balanced NPK fertilizer (15:15:15) for best results, with additional nitrogen during the early vegetative growth phase.

🌾 Average Yield: Maize yields around 2–4 tons per hectare, depending on soil quality, irrigation, and management practices.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure adequate spacing between plants for proper airflow and sunlight.
  • Regularly monitor for pest infestations and treat them early.
  • Practice crop rotation to maintain soil fertility and reduce disease risk.
  • Apply organic matter like compost to improve soil structure.
