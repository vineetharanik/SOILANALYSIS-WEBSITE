Identification

Grasshoppers are large insects, often green or brown, with powerful hind legs that they use for jumping. Their bodies can be 1-2 inches long, and they have long antennae and strong chewing mouthparts.

Signs of Infestation

  • Damage to plant leaves with ragged edges.
  • Visible grasshoppers on or near plants, especially during the day.
  • Leaves or stems that are partially or fully consumed by the pests.

Prevention & Control

Here are several methods to control and prevent grasshopper infestations:

  • Hand Picking: Physically remove grasshoppers from plants, especially in small-scale gardens.
  • Neem Oil: Use neem oil to disrupt the growth and reproduction of grasshoppers.
  • Insecticides: Apply insecticides such as carbaryl or permethrin if the infestation is severe.
  • Row Covers: Protect vulnerable crops by using floating row covers to prevent grasshoppers from reaching them.

Preventing Grasshoppers in the Future

  • Use natural predators like birds or lizards to help control grasshopper populations.
  • Use insecticidal soaps or diatomaceous earth as preventative measures early in the season.
  • Maintain good garden hygiene to avoid creating environments that attract grasshoppers.
