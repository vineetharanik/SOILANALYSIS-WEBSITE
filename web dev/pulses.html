🌱 Pulses - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Pulses grow well in red soil with good drainage. They can be grown in moderately fertile soils with a slightly acidic to neutral pH.

☀️ Climate: Pulses grow best in regions with moderate temperatures ranging from 20°C to 30°C. They need a dry climate during the maturity stage.

💧 Water Requirements: Pulses require minimal irrigation, but they do need adequate moisture during the early growth stages. They are tolerant to drought conditions.

📅 Growing Season: Pulses are typically grown during the Rabi season, with sowing in October–November and harvesting in February–March, depending on the variety.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Pulses are usually harvested within 4–5 months after sowing.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Common pests include pod borers and aphids. Pulses are also prone to fungal diseases like rust and wilt.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Pulses do not require much fertilization but benefit from a small amount of phosphorus. Organic manure can improve soil fertility.

🌾 Average Yield: Pulses yield 5–8 quintals per hectare, depending on the variety and growing conditions.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Proper crop rotation improves soil health and reduces pest buildup.
  • Ensure proper weed control, especially during the early growth stages.
  • Choose disease-resistant varieties to reduce the risk of crop loss.
  • Monitor soil moisture regularly to prevent water stress during the flowering stage.
