🌱 Wheat - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Wheat grows best in well-drained alluvial soil rich in organic matter. It prefers slightly acidic to neutral soil with good fertility.

☀️ Climate: Wheat requires a temperate climate with moderate rainfall. Ideal temperature ranges from 12°C to 25°C during the growing season.

💧 Water Requirements: Wheat needs moderate water during its growth. It can tolerate drought conditions but performs best with consistent moisture.

📅 Growing Season: Wheat is usually grown in the Rabi season, with sowing in November–December and harvesting in March–April, after 4–5 months.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Wheat matures in about 4–5 months after sowing, depending on the variety and climate conditions.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Wheat is susceptible to pests like aphids, wheat stem borers, and termites. Common diseases include rust, powdery mildew, and yellow rust.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Apply a balanced NPK fertilizer for wheat, with a higher amount of phosphorus to promote root growth and a balanced nitrogen-phosphorus-potassium ratio.

🌾 Average Yield: Wheat yields around 20–25 quintals per hectare under optimal conditions.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure timely sowing and harvesting to prevent weather-related losses.
  • Maintain proper soil moisture for optimal growth and yield.
  • Use disease-resistant wheat varieties to reduce the risk of infections.
  • Incorporate organic manure to enhance soil health and fertility.
