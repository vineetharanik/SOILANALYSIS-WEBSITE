🌱 Rice - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Rice thrives in alluvial soil with good water retention and fertility. It prefers slightly acidic to neutral soil with plenty of organic matter.

☀️ Climate: Rice requires a warm climate with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 35°C. It is suited for areas with high rainfall or where irrigation is available.

💧 Water Requirements: Rice needs abundant water. It requires a flooded field (paddy field) for optimal growth. Consistent water supply during the growing season is essential.

📅 Growing Season: Rice is typically grown in the Kharif season, with sowing in June–July and harvesting in October–November, after 4–6 months.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Rice matures in about 4–6 months, depending on the variety and growing conditions.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Common pests include rice weevils, brown plant hopper, and rice stem borers. Rice can also be affected by fungal diseases like rice blast and sheath blight.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Rice requires a balanced NPK fertilizer, with a higher dose of nitrogen for vegetative growth. Use compost or green manure to enhance soil fertility.

🌾 Average Yield: Rice yields 20–30 quintals per hectare under good management.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure proper water management with controlled flooding during the growing season.
  • Use disease-resistant varieties to reduce the risk of infections.
  • Regularly monitor for pests and apply appropriate pest control measures.
  • Incorporate organic matter to enhance soil fertility.
