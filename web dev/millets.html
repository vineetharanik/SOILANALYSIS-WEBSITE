🌱 Millets - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Millets grow well in red and black soil. They prefer soils that are well-drained and have low to medium fertility.

☀️ Climate: Millets thrive in hot climates with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 30°C. They are well-suited for semi-arid regions.

💧 Water Requirements: Millets are drought-tolerant and require minimal water. They are ideal for dryland farming.

📅 Growing Season: Millets are typically grown in the Kharif season, with sowing in June–July and harvesting in October–November.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Millets are ready for harvest in about 90–120 days, depending on the variety.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Millets are less susceptible to pests but can be affected by aphids and birds. Fungal diseases like downy mildew can also occur.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Millets require very little fertilizer. Apply a small amount of nitrogen-based fertilizer during the initial growth stages.

🌾 Average Yield: The average yield for millets is 8–10 quintals per hectare.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure good soil preparation for uniform germination.
  • Control weeds early to minimize competition.
  • Provide adequate irrigation during dry spells.
  • Plant at the correct depth for proper germination.
