Mites are tiny arachnids that can cause extensive damage to crops, particularly by sucking out plant juices. Here's how to manage them:

Identification

Mites are often too small to see with the naked eye, but their damage is noticeable. They are usually found on the undersides of leaves and can appear as tiny moving specks. Spider mites, a common variety, have a red or green color.

Signs of Infestation

  • Yellow stippling on leaves due to feeding damage.
  • Webbing or fine silk threads around affected plants.
  • Leaves that curl, distort, or drop prematurely.

Prevention & Control

Mites are best controlled by:

  • Insecticidal Soap: Apply insecticidal soap directly on the mites to suffocate and kill them.
  • Predatory Mites: Release beneficial predatory mites like Phytoseiulus persimilis to reduce mite populations.
  • Water Spray: Spray plants with water to dislodge mites, especially during dry conditions.
  • Neem Oil: Use neem oil to prevent the spread of mites and disrupt their life cycle.

Preventing Mites in the Future

  • Keep the garden clean of debris, which can harbor mites.
  • Improve air circulation around plants to make it harder for mites to thrive.
  • Monitor your plants regularly for signs of mites and take early action if needed.
