🌱 Tea - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Tea grows best in slightly acidic, well-drained laterite soil. The soil should be rich in organic matter to promote healthy root development and high yields.

☀️ Climate: Tea thrives in cool, tropical to subtropical climates, with temperatures between 15°C to 30°C. It requires consistent rainfall and high humidity throughout the year.

💧 Water Requirements: Tea plants need abundant water, especially during the growing season. While they do not tolerate drought well, they must also be protected from waterlogging.

📅 Growing Season: Tea is grown year-round in areas with consistent rainfall. The ideal planting season is from March to May, and the first harvest is usually after 3–5 years of planting.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Tea plants take around 3–5 years to reach full production. The harvesting period generally starts in the 2nd or 3rd year and continues for several years.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Common pests include tea mosquito bugs, aphids, and caterpillars. Diseases like leaf blight and root rot can affect tea plants if not managed properly.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Use balanced fertilizers with micronutrients for optimal growth. Regular organic matter applications improve soil fertility and enhance tea leaf quality.

🌾 Average Yield: Tea bushes can yield 1–3 tons of leaves per hectare per year, depending on soil quality, climate, and management practices.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Prune tea bushes regularly to promote fresh growth and increase yield.
  • Ensure the plants are shaded from the midday sun to prevent leaf scorching.
  • Use organic mulching techniques to maintain moisture and control weeds.
