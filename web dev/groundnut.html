🌱 Groundnuts (Peanuts) - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Groundnuts grow well in red soil that is well-drained and sandy loam. It should have a neutral pH for optimal growth.

☀️ Climate: Groundnuts require a warm climate with temperatures ranging from 25°C to 35°C. It needs moderate rainfall for proper growth.

💧 Water Requirements: Groundnuts need moderate watering. Ensure the soil is well-drained to prevent waterlogging, as this can affect root development.

📅 Growing Season: Groundnuts are grown in the Kharif season, with sowing in June–July and harvesting after 4–5 months, usually in October–November.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Groundnuts mature in about 120–150 days, depending on the variety.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Aphids, whiteflies, and groundnut caterpillars are common pests. Fungal diseases like root rot and rust can also affect groundnuts.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Apply balanced NPK fertilizers in the ratio of 20:60:60. Incorporating organic manure can improve soil fertility.

🌾 Average Yield: Average yield is around 12–18 quintals per hectare with proper management.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Plant groundnut seeds at the correct depth for optimal germination.
  • Ensure proper spacing between plants to allow good airflow.
  • Regularly monitor for pests and apply organic pesticides as necessary.
  • Keep the soil free of weeds, especially during the early growth stages.
