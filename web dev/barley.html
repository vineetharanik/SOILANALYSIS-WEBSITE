🌾 Barley - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Barley thrives in well-drained, sandy loam soils with a pH of 6–7. It prefers soils with good organic matter content.

☀️ Climate: Barley grows well in temperate climates with temperatures ranging from 15°C to 20°C. It requires a cool, moist climate with moderate rainfall.

💧 Water Requirements: Barley requires moderate water, especially during the early stages of growth. It is more tolerant of drought compared to other crops.

📅 Growing Season: Barley is typically grown during the Rabi season. Sowing begins in October–November, and harvesting takes place in February–March.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Barley matures in about 4–5 months, depending on the variety.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Pests include aphids, barley weevils, and armyworms. Diseases like rust and powdery mildew are common in barley fields.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: Use a balanced fertilizer with NPK (20:20:20) or a nitrogen-rich fertilizer during the early growth stages.

🌾 Average Yield: Barley yields around 1.5–2.5 tons per hectare, depending on soil fertility, irrigation, and pest control.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure proper spacing to prevent overcrowding and allow sunlight penetration.
  • Practice crop rotation to avoid pest buildup and soil degradation.
  • Maintain soil pH between 6 and 7 for optimal growth.
