🌻 Oilseeds - Detailed Crop Information

📍 Soil Type: Oilseeds such as sunflower, mustard, and soybean grow best in loamy soils with a pH of 6–7. They prefer well-drained soils rich in organic matter.

☀️ Climate: Oilseeds thrive in warm climates with temperatures between 20°C to 30°C. Most oilseeds require a moderate rainfall between 400–800 mm annually.

💧 Water Requirements: Oilseeds require moderate water, with specific needs during the flowering and pod development stages.

📅 Growing Season: Oilseeds are typically grown during the Rabi or Kharif season, depending on the region. Sowing begins in October–November or April–May, with harvesting after 3–4 months.

⏳ Time to Harvest: Oilseeds are ready for harvest in 3–4 months, depending on the variety and climate conditions.

🐛 Common Pests & Diseases: Pests include aphids, caterpillars, and whiteflies. Diseases such as rust and powdery mildew can also affect the crop.

💊 Fertilizer Suggestions: A balanced NPK fertilizer (20:20:20) is ideal for oilseeds, with an additional dose of phosphorus during flowering.

🌾 Average Yield: Oilseeds yield around 1–2 tons per hectare, depending on crop type, soil quality, and weather conditions.

✅ Tips for Better Growth:

  • Ensure proper irrigation, especially during the flowering period.
  • Apply organic matter to improve soil health and increase yields.
  • Monitor for pests regularly and take preventive measures.
